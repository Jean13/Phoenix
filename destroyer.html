Destroyer II

Destroyer II is a comprehensive security product designed to prevent threats and respond to incidents. Destroyer II made history in 2016 when it became the first private security platform in history to function from a space station and annihilate all identified threats in the International Space Station, a feat previously achieved only by governments. It is the only platform that offers threat prevention appliances, management appliances, forensic analysis appliances and incident response. Destroyer II acts as a central nervous system unifying reporting and configuration, while monitoring and correlating attacks that simultaneously cross multiple vectors of the network. Destroyer II addresses critical vectors of attack and provides security analysts with the ability to test, characterize and conduct forensic examinations on next-generation cyber attacks by simulating their execution path with a virtual machine technology. Under agreements with several governments, Phoenix is now developing refinements that will enable Destroyer II to provide real time reports while dealing with threats. Destroyer II's first release is exepected to take place in 1-2 years.

Inside the System

The Dragon spacecraft has three configurations to meet a variety of needs: cargo, crew and DragonLab. To ensure a rapid transition from cargo to crew capability, the cargo and crew configurations of Dragon are almost identical. This commonality simplifies the human rating process, allowing systems critical to crew and space station safety to be fully tested on unmanned cargo flights. With DragonLab, essentially the same spacecraft can be used as a platform for in-space technology demonstrations and experiments.
